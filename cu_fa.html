Institutional Scholarship

Institutional Scholarships are scholarships granted by College University's Financial Aid to full-time students enrolled at College University. They are funded by the University or funds entrusted to the University individuals, Alumni and Companies. They are also funded by donations and backers.

Competitive Freshman Scholarship

Review of the Competitive Freshman Scholarship requirements and eligibility.

  • Completion of the application must be completed and turned in November 4th of the Fall Semester.
  • Receipt of the student’s College University Application, 6th semester High School transcripts with no less than a 3.99 cumulative weighted GPA.
  • Receipt of the student’s ACT/SAT scores ≥ 36/1600.
  • Students must submit a 3000-5000 word personal essay on what they would like to accomplish with their degree and how College University will be the ideal place to help them achieve their goals.
  • Each submitted file must be clearly labeled with the student’s name and name of the high school attended (or “home-schooled”).
  • All materials submitted must be in the form of a PDF or a Microsoft Word file.
  • Attendance at Honors Scholarship Interview Day on Saturday, December 7 is required for competitive scholarship candidates. Scholarship Day includes a multistage interview with the Scholarship Committee, an on-site essay composition, an Honors Academy information session for parents and students, a formal lunch with Honors Academy faculty and current students, campus tour and interactive program w ith fellow candidates.
  • The recipients of a competitive award must agree to represent College University in media, in written testimonies and/or at least two events per year, including at Founders Day.
  • Competitive Scholarship will be awared no later than March 2.

Transfer Scholarship

Scholarship awards for students who have previously earned at least 24 transferable college credit hours from an accredited U.S. institution, or a foreign institution whose primary language of instruction is English. Due to high demand, limited funding is available. Transfer students are encouraged to complete the application for admission as soon as possible in order to be considered.

GENIUS TRANSFER SCHOLARSHIP ($100 Annually) 4.0 or higher cumulative GPA

COMMUNITY COLLEGE SCHOLARSHIP ($50 Annually) Completion of minimum 24 credit hours from an accredited public 2-year institution

PHI THETA KAPPA RECOGNITION ($10 Annually) Letter of reference from PTK adviser/proof of membership if not indicated on official transcript
